Building an Enclosure for a Hypercube Evo Printer

Проблема

A user with a Hypercube Evo printer wants to build an enclosure and is asking for advice on suitable designs and materials. They are concerned about whether plastic panels can withstand the heat inside the chamber.

Рекомендація (не підтверджено)

For a cube-style printer like the Hypercube, it is recommended to attach panels directly to the aluminum extrusions. Users suggested looking at designs from the VzBot Github or using modified VCore 3 plates, which mount with t-nuts. Regarding materials, polycarbonate sheets are recommended over metal. Metal is a poor choice as it will conduct heat out of the chamber. Polycarbonate is suitable for the typical chamber temperatures required for ABS and ASA (60-70°C), as its glass transition temperature is much higher at 147°C. For most use cases, the goal is simply to shield the printer from room temperature fluctuations. For extremely high-temperature engineering filaments like Ultem (PEI), which require chamber temperatures over 100°C, special considerations are needed, such as a special bed surface (as it can fuse to regular PEI beds) and extensive filament drying (e.g., 120°C for 8 hours).
