Troubleshooting a Wobbly Bed Mesh with Biqu Microprobe in Klipper

Case e0675ce64232 · 3dprinting

#Klipper#Bed Leveling#Bed Mesh#Biqu Microprobe#Probe#Configuration#Troubleshooting

Проблема

A user switched from an inductive probe to a Biqu microprobe and is getting a "wobbly" or uneven bed mesh, as seen in the heightmap visualization. They are asking if this is normal and how to improve it after the change.

Рекомендація (не підтверджено)

It was recommended to first ensure the probe is mounted solidly. Then, several Klipper configuration changes were suggested for the `[probe]` and `[bed_mesh]` sections. For `[probe]`: `deactivate_on_each_sample: False`, `samples_result: median`, `samples: 4`, `samples_tolerance: 0.06`, `samples_tolerance_retries: 5`, `sample_retract_dist: 5`. For `[bed_mesh]`: `probe_count: 3`, `algorithm: lagrange`. It was also critically important to heat the bed to temperature before probing. Further analysis from other users suggested the wavy pattern could be a mechanical issue with the X-axis (e.g., a loose carriage or a wonky wheel on the Elegoo Neptune 3 Pro). The user was advised to check and tighten the X-axis. It was also noted that a 12x12 probe grid is likely overkill, and a 3x3 grid over the bed mount points is better for initial leveling.
